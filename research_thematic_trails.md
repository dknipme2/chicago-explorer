### CHINESE: Xiao Long Bao (Soup Dumplings)

**Hing Kee Restaurant**
- Address: 2140 S Archer Ave, Chicago, IL 60616
- Neighborhood: Chinatown
- Lat/Lng: 41.8535, -87.6330
- What makes it special: Widely considered the best soup dumplings in Chicago. Three varieties: pork, crab and pork, and chicken. Delicate chewy wrappers brimming with warm, flavorful broth. BYOB.
- Price: $$ (~$15-25/person)
- MUST-TRY: Crab and pork xiao long bao
- Tags: xiao-long-bao, chinatown, soup-dumplings, byob

**Qing Xiang Yuan Dumplings (QXY)**
- Address: 2002 S Wentworth Ave #103, Chicago, IL 60616
- Neighborhood: Chinatown
- Lat/Lng: 41.8550, -87.6317
- What makes it special: Minimalist-chic BYOB with artisanal dumplings made to order. Beyond standard pork — they offer lamb and coriander, beef and celery, crab roe, and even sea urchin varieties. Every dumpling is hand-folded fresh.
- Price: $$ (~$15-25/person)
- MUST-TRY: Lamb and coriander dumplings
- Tags: artisan-dumplings, chinatown, byob, creative-flavors

### CHINESE: Har Gow & Shu Mai (Dim Sum Dumplings)

**Dolo Restaurant and Bar**
- Address: 2222 S Archer Ave, Chicago, IL 60616
- Neighborhood: Chinatown
- Lat/Lng: 41.8530, -87.6340
- What makes it special: Modern dim sum dining room with both cart service and table ordering. All dim sum made fresh daily. Classic siu mai and har gow are excellent, but the creative dishes (like truffle dumplings) set it apart. Full bar unlike most dim sum spots.
- Price: $$ (~$25-35/person)
- MUST-TRY: Har gow (shrimp dumplings) plus the BBQ pork buns
- Tags: dim-sum, chinatown, modern, full-bar

**Phoenix Restaurant** (already in database)
- Address: 2131 S Archer Ave, Chicago, IL 60616
- Neighborhood: Chinatown
- Lat/Lng: 41.8537, -87.6325
- What makes it special: Classic cart-service dim sum in a massive 500-seat dining room. Har gow, siu mai, chicken feet, crispy chive dumplings. The cart experience is a dying art. Weekend brunch is the move.
- Price: $$ (~$22/person)
- MUST-TRY: Har gow from the cart, plus crispy chive dumplings
- Tags: dim-sum, cart-service, weekend-brunch

**MingHin Cuisine**
- Address: 2168 S Archer Ave, Chicago, IL 60616
- Neighborhood: Chinatown
- Lat/Lng: 41.8533, -87.6335
- What makes it special: Michelin Bib Gourmand Cantonese with outstanding dim sum. Traditional siu mai, lotus-wrapped sticky rice, and some of the best creamy egg yolk buns in the city. Open until midnight.
- Price: $$ (~$25-35/person)
- MUST-TRY: Creamy egg yolk buns (liu sha bao)
- Tags: dim-sum, bib-gourmand, cantonese, late-night

### UKRAINIAN: Varenyky

**Tryzub Ukrainian Kitchen**
- Address: 2201 W Chicago Ave, Chicago, IL 60622
- Neighborhood: Ukrainian Village
- Lat/Lng: 41.8967, -87.6810
- What makes it special: Opened in 2016 as a cultural and culinary gem. Everything handmade. The varenyky party platter features 16 dumplings in four varieties: potato, sauerkraut and mushroom, and more. The building itself has the world's largest tryzub (Ukrainian national symbol) built into its brickwork, plus thousand-year-old artifacts inside.
- Price: $$ (~$18-28/person)
- MUST-TRY: Varenyky party platter (16 pcs, 4 varieties)
- Tags: varenyky, ukrainian-village, cultural-experience, handmade

### NEPALI: Momos

**The Momo World**
- Address: 2535 W Devon Ave, Chicago, IL 60659 (approximate)
- Neighborhood: West Ridge (Devon Ave)
- Lat/Lng: 41.9977, -87.6920

- Price: $ (~$10-15/person)
- MUST-TRY: Jhol momos (momos bathed in Himalayan creamy soup)
- Tags: momos, nepali, devon-ave, specialty

**Himalayan Sherpa Kitchen**
- Address: 2701 W Lawrence Ave, Chicago, IL 60625
- Neighborhood: Lincoln Square
- Lat/Lng: 41.9317, -87.6952

- Price: $$ (~$15-22/person)
- MUST-TRY: Steamed chicken momos with tomato achar
- Tags: momos, nepali, tibetan, sherpa, lincoln-square

### Vietnamese Pho

**Pho 777**
- Address: 1065 W Argyle St, Chicago, IL 60640
- Neighborhood: Uptown (Little Saigon / Argyle)
- Lat/Lng: 41.9733, -87.6590
- What makes it special: Cornerstone of the West Argyle Historic District. Lighter, sweeter broth profile that purists love. Generous portions, consistent quality, decades of reputation. A pillar of Chicago's Little Saigon.
- Price: $ (~$12-15/person)
- MUST-TRY: Large combination pho (pho dac biet)
- Tags: pho, argyle, little-saigon, institution

**Hai Yen**
- Address: 1055 W Argyle St, Chicago, IL 60640
- Neighborhood: Uptown (Little Saigon)
- Lat/Lng: 41.9733, -87.6588
- What makes it special: Highest-rated Vietnamese spot on Argyle with an 8.2/10 score. Excellent pho plus a broader Vietnamese menu including claypot dishes and fresh spring rolls.
- Price: $ (~$12-18/person)
- MUST-TRY: Pho tai (rare beef pho)
- Tags: pho, argyle, top-rated

### Japanese Ramen

**Ramen Wasabi**
- Address: 2101 N Milwaukee Ave, Chicago, IL 60647 (Logan Square) + Fulton Market location
- Neighborhood: Logan Square
- Lat/Lng: 41.9200, -87.6930
- What makes it special: No-frills, no-trends ramen. The tonkotsu broth is made from Berkshire pork bones in a 45+ hour process. Subtly spiced, rich, cloudy with pork fat. Simple and unembellished — lets the broth speak.
- Price: $$ (~$16-22/person)
- MUST-TRY: Tonkotsu ramen
- Tags: ramen, tonkotsu, logan-square, berkshire-pork

**Akahoshi Ramen**
- Address: 3067 N Milwaukee Ave, Chicago, IL 60618
- Neighborhood: Logan Square
- Lat/Lng: 41.9360, -87.7100
- What makes it special: From "Ramen Lord" Mike Satinover. Evolved from a cult pop-up. Reservations booked five weeks out. Experiments like Midwest Shoyu (thin flat noodles, katsuobushi, pork chashu) and the Akahoshi Miso with Sapporo-style crinkly noodles. This is ramen as craft.
- Price: $$ (~$18-25/person)
- MUST-TRY: Akahoshi Miso (signature)
- Tags: ramen, craft, logan-square, reservation-required

**High Five Ramen**
- Address: 112 N Green St (basement of Green Street Smoked Meats), Chicago, IL 60607
- Neighborhood: West Loop
- Lat/Lng: 41.8844, -87.6489
- What makes it special: Hidden in a basement below a BBQ joint. Dark, tiny, intense. Signature bowl: thick noodles, soft-boiled egg, buttery chashu, and an incredibly spicy tonkotsu broth. One of Chicago's most unique ramen experiences.
- Price: $$ (~$18-22/person)
- MUST-TRY: Spicy tonkotsu (the only way)
- Tags: ramen, spicy, hidden, west-loop, basement

**Hokkaido Ramen Santouka** (at Mitsuwa)
- Address: 100 E Algonquin Rd, Arlington Heights, IL 60005
- Neighborhood: Arlington Heights (inside Mitsuwa Marketplace)
- Lat/Lng: 41.9627, -87.9780
- What makes it special: Chicago outpost of a revered Hokkaido ramen chain. The shio (salt) ramen is their specialty — a clear, deeply savory pork broth that's lighter than tonkotsu but incredibly satisfying. Pair with Mitsuwa grocery shopping.
- Price: $$ (~$15-20/person)
- MUST-TRY: Shio (salt) ramen with toroniku (pork cheek)
- Tags: ramen, hokkaido, shio, mitsuwa, suburban

### Chinese Hand-Pulled Noodles

**Slurp Slurp Noodles**
- Address: 2247 S Wentworth Ave, Chicago, IL 60616
- Neighborhood: Chinatown
- Lat/Lng: 41.8510, -87.6317

- MUST-TRY: Hand-pulled noodles in beef broth
- Tags: hand-pulled, chinatown, noodle-show

**Xi'an Cuisine**
- Address: 225 W Cermak Rd, Chicago, IL 60616 (Chinatown) + 769 W Jackson Blvd (West Loop)
- Neighborhood: Chinatown / West Loop
- Lat/Lng: 41.8525, -87.6335 (Chinatown)
- What makes it special: Traditional dishes from the ancient city of Xi'an. The biang biang mian (hand-pulled belt noodles) are wide, chewy, and spectacular. Noodles are stretched on-site. The cumin lamb hand-pulled noodles are a signature of Xi'an cuisine.
- Price: $ (~$12-18/person)
- MUST-TRY: Biang biang mian (hand-ripped belt noodles) with cumin lamb
- Tags: hand-pulled, xian, biang-biang, chinatown, west-loop

## TRAIL 3: FERMENTATION STATION

*Kimchi, pickles, funky cheese, kombucha, and everything cultured.*

---

### Fermented Foods Producers

**Vargo Brother Ferments**
- Address: Chicago (production facility / farmers markets)
- Neighborhood: various markets
- Lat/Lng: 41.8900, -87.6500 (approximate, varies by market)
- What makes it special: Chicago's local Black-owned pickle shop run by Chefs Sebastian Vargo and Taylor Hanna. Small-batch fermented condiments, jams, krauts, kimchi, and kombucha. Find them at farmers markets and specialty retailers.
- Price: $ (~$8-15 per jar)
- MUST-TRY: Their house kimchi
- Tags: fermented, kimchi, local, black-owned, farmers-market

**Sidekick Ferments** (at Green City Market)
- Address: Green City Market, Chicago (Lincoln Park + West Loop locations)
- Neighborhood: Lincoln Park / West Loop
- Lat/Lng: 41.9230, -87.6360 (Lincoln Park market)
- What makes it special: Small-batch kosher-style dills, kimchi, sauerkraut, and lacto-fermented vegetables sold at Chicago's premier farmers market. Everything fermented with care for gut health.
- Price: $ (~$8-12 per jar)
- MUST-TRY: Kosher dill pickles
- Tags: fermented, pickles, farmers-market, small-batch

### Kimchi at Korean Markets

**Joong Boo Market**
- Address: 3333 N Kimball Ave, Chicago, IL 60618
- Neighborhood: Avondale
- Lat/Lng: 41.9437, -87.7134
- What makes it special: Chicago's premier Asian grocery since 1992. Has a whole aisle dedicated to kimchi varieties — dozens of brands, styles (napa cabbage, radish, cucumber, water kimchi), and fermentation levels. Also has an in-house food court. Wednesday/Friday $30 sashimi platter specials.
- Price: $ (~$5-15 per container)
- MUST-TRY: Browse the kimchi aisle and grab both fresh (young) and aged (muk eun ji) kimchi
- Tags: kimchi, korean-market, kimchi-aisle, avondale

**H Mart Naperville**
- Address: 1295 E Ogden Ave, Naperville, IL 60563
- Neighborhood: Naperville (suburb)
- Lat/Lng: 41.7749, -88.1175
- What makes it special: Closest major Korean market to Glen Ellyn (~15 min). Extensive kimchi section with multiple Korean brands. In-store food court for Korean food. Full prepared foods section.
- Price: $ (~$5-15 per container)
- MUST-TRY: Tobagi brand whole napa cabbage kimchi
- Tags: kimchi, h-mart, suburban, close-to-base

### Kombucha

**The Kombucha Room**
- Address: Chicago (check current location)

### Cheese Shops (Aged / Funky)

**Beautiful Rind**
- Address: 2211 N Milwaukee Ave, Chicago, IL 60647
- Neighborhood: Logan Square
- Lat/Lng: 41.9217, -87.6950

- Price: $$ (~$12-25 for cheese selections)
- MUST-TRY: Jasper Hill Farm Harbison (bark-wrapped, oozy, funky)

**Cheese & Board**
- Address: West Loop, Chicago (opened 2025)
- Neighborhood: West Loop
- Lat/Lng: 41.8846, -87.6520 (approximate)

- Price: $$ (~$15-30 for boards)
- MUST-TRY: Old Witch aged cow cheese
- Tags: cheese-shop, aged-cheese, west-loop, curated

### Fermented Hot Sauce

> **NOTE:** Chicago doesn't have a dedicated fermented hot sauce shop, but Small Axe Peppers (carried at many local shops) makes fermented hot sauces from urban-grown peppers. ### Ethiopian (Injera Platters)

**Demera Ethiopian Restaurant** (already in database)
- Address: 4801 N Broadway, Chicago, IL 60640
- Neighborhood: Uptown
- Lat/Lng: 41.9683, -87.6601
- What makes it special: Michelin-noted. Communal injera platters with doro wot, tibs, kitfo. Chef-curated messob plates. Eat everything with torn pieces of spongy injera bread.
- Price: $$ (~$25/person)
- MUST-TRY: Chef's sampler platter for two (covers the greatest hits)

**Tesfa Ethiopian Cuisine**
- Address: 5352 N Broadway, Chicago, IL 60640
- Neighborhood: Edgewater
- Lat/Lng: 41.9795, -87.6600
- What makes it special: Three different types of house-made injera, all described as exceptional. Doro wot, spicy catfish, and a range of vegetarian stews. More intimate than Demera, equally authentic.
- Price: $$ (~$20-28/person)
- MUST-TRY: Doro wot with Terefech's signature injera
- Tags: ethiopian, injera, edgewater, handmade-injera

**Ethiopian Diamond**
- Address: 6120 N Broadway, Chicago, IL 60660
- Neighborhood: Edgewater
- Lat/Lng: 41.9930, -87.6600
- What makes it special: The oldest Ethiopian spot in Chicago, nearly three decades running. Flaky sambusas, stews with signature "Diamond berbere" spice blend. Institution status.
- Price: $$ (~$20-25/person)
- MUST-TRY: Diamond berbere-spiced wot platter
- Tags: ethiopian, institution, oldest, diamond-berbere
